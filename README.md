## Why these two projects

Market entry and profitability diagnostics are the two most common case types
used in management consulting interviews. Building one of each — rather than
one project done twice — was a deliberate choice to show range across the
skill set: market sizing and unit economics in Project 1, financial statement
analysis and comparative benchmarking in Project 2.

## Methodology

Both projects follow a hypothesis-driven, MECE structure:

1. Frame the business question as a small set of mutually exclusive,
   collectively exhaustive hypotheses
2. Ground every hypothesis in real, cited public data — industry reports,
   company financial disclosures, and analyst commentary — rather than
   assumed figures
3. Build a financial model (Excel) that supports the recommendation with
   numbers, not just narrative
4. Communicate the finding as a structured case deck with one governing
   thought per slide, in the style used by top consulting firms

## Tools used

Excel (financial modeling, formulas, scenario inputs) · PowerPoint
(case deck design) · Public company filings and industry research
(market/financial data)

## A note on scope and honesty

These are **independent, self-directed portfolio projects**, not real client
engagements. Company names in Project 1 ("FreshGo Retail") are disguised
fictional clients used to frame a market-entry exercise, in the same way real
consulting case interviews use disguised client names. Project 2 uses the
real, publicly reported financials of SpiceJet and IndiGo — every figure is
cited to its source in the appendix of the deck and in the Excel workbook.
Sources and any known cross-source rounding differences are documented in
each project's own README and in the appendix slide of each deck.
