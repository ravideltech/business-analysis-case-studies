# SpiceJet: Diagnosing a Profitability Crisis

**Case type:** Profitability Diagnostic
**Subject:** SpiceJet Ltd. (real company, public financials), benchmarked
against IndiGo (InterGlobe Aviation)
**Question:** Why did SpiceJet's FY26 quarterly results swing sharply into
loss, and what should the airline do about it?

## Finding (governing thought)

Losses are a capacity story first, a cost story second. Revenue collapsed
faster than costs could adjust in Q1 FY26 (an industry-wide Pratt & Whitney
engine grounding crisis cut capacity across every major carrier), then
recovered faster than costs grew in Q3 — the loss line tracks the revenue
curve almost exactly. SpiceJet's real disadvantage isn't cost discipline;
it's scale. At roughly 1/17th IndiGo's quarterly revenue, SpiceJet has no
margin cushion to absorb shocks that IndiGo absorbs within a 22.9% operating
margin.

## Key figures used

- SpiceJet revenue: ₹1,060cr (Q1 FY26, -35.6% YoY) → ₹708cr (Q2, trough) →
  ₹1,408cr (Q3, +13.8% YoY)
- SpiceJet net result: -₹234cr → -₹621cr → -₹261cr over the same quarters
- IndiGo Q3 FY26 benchmark: ₹23,472cr revenue, +22.9% operating margin,
  ₹550cr net profit despite the same labour-code and forex headwinds
